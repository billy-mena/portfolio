01 — The Strategic Objective

This research was commissioned by the United Nations Volunteers (UNV) APEC Division to bridge a critical gap: commercial shrimp farming interests and mangrove conservation were in direct conflict across developing regions — with no consolidated, accessible resource to inform policy or sustainable practice.

The objective was to produce a survey-level technical report that synthesized fragmented international research into a coherent, actionable knowledge base — one that could serve government agencies, NGOs, and small-scale farming communities simultaneously.

"The ability to master niche scientific domains to produce peer-reviewed-level resources for international organizations — without institutional support — demonstrates the exact kind of adaptive research capability that defines a world-class knowledge worker."

Self-assessment · UNV Research Assignment, 2003–2005
02 — The Architecture
🗂️
Complex Data Management & Source Synthesis
Synthesized environmental, social, and economic variables from 30+ global peer-reviewed sources and NGO reports. Identified "High-Yield, Low-Impact" aquaculture models across Southeast Asia, South America, and West Africa — translating technical findings into policy-ready language for non-specialist stakeholders.
✍️
Technical Writing & Policy Mapping
Produced a structured 25-page report covering the environmental impact of mangrove conversion, economic viability models for integrated shrimp-mangrove systems, regulatory frameworks across developing nations, and community-based rehabilitation strategies. Written to UN documentation standards — accessible to both policy makers and field practitioners.
🔬
Self-Taught Research Methodology
Mastered aquaculture science, mangrove ecology, and international environmental policy frameworks from scratch — demonstrating the capacity to rapidly develop domain expertise in specialized technical fields to produce peer-reviewed-level outputs for an international NGO without institutional training.
